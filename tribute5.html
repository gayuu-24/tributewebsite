Legacy

Augusta Fells Savage Institute of Visual Arts (Baltimore, MD):

Named after Augusta Savage, the school emphasizes her commitment to art education and her influence on future generations of artists. It aims to cultivate the same spirit of creativity and excellence that Savage embodied throughout her career.

Augusta Savage Friendship Park (Green Cove Springs, FL):

The park’s name celebrates Savage’s legacy and her connection to the community. It provides a place for residents to gather and enjoy recreational activities while commemorating her impact on art and culture.

Augusta Savage Arts and Cultural Center (Green Cove Springs, FL):

The center honors Augusta Savage by fostering a vibrant cultural environment and supporting artistic expression. It reflects her belief in the importance of art and culture in enriching communities and educating future generations.

Augusta Savage Gallery at the University of Massachusetts Amherst:

The gallery is named in honor of Augusta Savage to recognize her contributions to art and education. It underscores her role in promoting the visibility of African American artists and her influence on the art world.

Death

Augusta Savage passed away on March 26, 1962. Despite facing numerous challenges throughout her life, including racial and gender discrimination and following a long bout with cancer. she left a profound legacy in the art world. Her work continues to inspire and influence artists and art educators.
